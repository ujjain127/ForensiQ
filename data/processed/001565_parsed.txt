Each person authorized by this Order shall present a copy of this Order to security 
personnel each time the person enters the Courthouse with such equipment.  Courthouse 
security personnel will require the authorized person to present a valid Bar Identification, if 
applicable, as well as picture identification at the time of entry.   
The equipment is subject to inspection at any time by courthouse security personnel.  
Equipment shall not be fitted with video or audio recording devices and must have all sound 
muted while in use in the courtroom.  The equipment may not be connected to the Judiciary’s 
computer network within the Courthouse.  Pending a determination of court-wide policy, the 
judge presiding over a particular matter, in his or her discretion, will determine if internet 
access will be allowed in that judge's courtroom on a case-by-case basis. 
The Clerk of the Court shall ensure a copy of this order is provided to appropriate 
security personnel and to each person authorized use of a notebook computer while in the 
Courthouse. 
This Order replaces Administrative Order TPA-2008-11, which shall be archived.   
This Order is effective January 1, 2009 and shall expire at midnight on February 1, 
2009. 
DONE and ORDERED in Tampa, Florida, this 24th day of December, 2008.
